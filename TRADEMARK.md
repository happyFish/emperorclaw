# Trademark Policy

"Emperor Claw" and the Emperor Claw logo are trademarks of Malecu OU. The
source code license ([FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0](./LICENSE)) covers the code — it
does not grant rights to the name or the logo. This policy explains what you
can and can't do with them.

## You are welcome to (no permission needed)

- Say your product or service **"works with Emperor Claw"**, **"is built on
  Emperor Claw"**, or **"is powered by Emperor Claw"** — truthful, referential
  use is always fine.
- Run a self-hosted instance for yourself, your company, or your clients and
  call it what it is: an Emperor Claw instance.
- Use the name in articles, talks, tutorials, reviews, comparisons, and
  academic work.
- Keep the name in unmodified redistributions of the official releases.

## You need a different name for

- **A fork you distribute publicly.** Modified versions must be renamed and
  rebranded (logo included) so users aren't confused about what they're
  installing or who maintains it. "Based on Emperor Claw" attribution is
  welcome.
- **Any commercial product or hosted service.** You may not offer anything
  called "Emperor Claw", "EmperorClaw Cloud", or any confusingly similar name
  ("Emperor Claw Pro", "EmprClaw", etc.). The only party who may operate a
  service under this name is Malecu OU.
- **Domain names, company names, app-store listings, and social accounts**
  that lead with the mark or imply official status.

## Logo files

Logo assets in this repository are provided so you can *refer to* the genuine
project (e.g., a "powered by" badge or an integration tile). They may not be
used as the identity of another product or service.

## Questions

If you're unsure whether a use is OK, it probably needs a quick yes — open a
GitHub issue or contact the maintainer. Good-faith community use will always
get a generous reading; the policy exists to stop impersonation, not
enthusiasm.
