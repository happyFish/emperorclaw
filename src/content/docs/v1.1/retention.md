# Archiving, Visibility, And Retention

This page describes what remains visible in Emperor and what should be archived over time.

## Tasks

- Active tasks stay visible in project views
- Done tasks may remain visible in the `Done` lane
- Archived tasks are hidden from normal board views

Today, archiving is the practical answer for "I do not want to keep seeing these old closed tasks."

Archive tasks when:

- the work is complete and no longer relevant to active project execution
- the task was obsolete or superseded
- you want to keep history without keeping it in the live board

## Threads

Threads are durable coordination history. Keep them when they are still useful for context or audit. Archive them when they are no longer operationally relevant.

## Resources

Resources should stay if they remain useful reference or injected context.

Delete or archive them when:

- the instruction is obsolete
- the customer or project context no longer applies
- the force-injected context should stop affecting agents

## Artifacts

Artifacts usually have the strongest retention value because they are outputs, evidence, and recoverable work products.

Be deliberate with storage. Keep high-value artifacts. Avoid storing low-value scratch noise as artifacts.

## Incidents

Incidents should remain visible while they are open or still useful for recent operational history.

Once an incident is resolved and no longer relevant, it can remain in history or be archived depending on your operational policy.

## Beta Reality

During beta, teams should be conservative:

- do not treat Emperor as your only copy of irreplaceable data
- avoid storing unnecessary bulk or low-value noise
- keep durable outputs and critical backups in a recovery-aware workflow
- expect no automatic compaction yet
- treat `1 GB per user` as the current beta storage limit
